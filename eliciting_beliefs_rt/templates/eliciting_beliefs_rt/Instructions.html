{% blocktrans trimmed with n=11 %} The basic idea
The third task is a judicial dialogue game. In this game, you are randomly assigned to a role (international or domestic court) at the beginning of the game and anonymously matched with another player in this room. If for instance, you are assigned to "international court", you will be match with another person who's role is "domestic court". You will keep your role and your partner until the end of the game. You will have to make decisions, which can be adjusted during the experiment. Your earnings accumulate at a rate that depends on the action you choose and the action chosen by the other participants you are matched with. The details are explained below.

The sequence of the game

International court decides as a first mover between two actions, exert restrain (~A ) or be assertive (A). If the international court exert restrain (~A) the game stops and both players accrue equal payoffs (4;4). If the international court decides to be assertive (A), the domestic court responds to the action taken by international court by chosen the action not to challenge (~C) realising the payoffs (7;1) or to challenge (C) accruing equal payoffs (-4;-4). The corresponding payoffs are realized depending on the action chosen by domestic court and the game stops. All subjects have the same information. You are told the outcome of the game after each play, including their current partners' strategies.

This game will be repeated n rounds. The number of rounds is randomly drawn for each pair of players (international-domestic). This mean that one pair plays this game 10 times, another pair of players may repeat it only 5 times.

During each round, both players (international/domestic court) can gain additional money by guessing which action the other player is most likely to choose. We ask you, before or after submitting your decision choice, to move a slider between 0 (not likely) and 100 (very likely) expressing the likelihood that the other player chooses a specific action. If your guess is correct, you will earn additionally 0.15 Euro and otherwise you get 0.05 Euro. Imagine you are assigned to the international court, then after submitting your decision choice, you will be asked what is the likelihood that domestic court plays the action to challenge C. In case your guess is 95 and the domestic court chooses the action not to challenge, you will earn additionally 0.15 Euro to the payoff in this round.

The final payoff of this game is the average money earned in the n number of rounds.

Summary:
- You will be assigned to a role (international court and domestic court)
- You will be anonymously matched with another player of different role
- Your role and partner won't change during the game
- You can gain additional money by guessing the right action of the other player
- The number of rounds is randomly drawn
- Your final payoff is the average money earned during the n rounds

Please raise your hand if you have any question about the game, a research assistant will come to answer your question in private.

Before starting the game, we assess your understanding about the game by participating in a short quiz of 2 questions. {% endblocktrans %}
